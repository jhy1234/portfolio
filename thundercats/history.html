History of Augmented Reality

AR as an idea is present from the early 20th century. L Frank Baum, the creator of the Wizard of Oz, wrote an illustrated novel in 1901 which imagines a type of augmented reality. Although the first modern system, using a mixture of AR and VR was invented in 1968 which featured a head mounted display to show simple graphics.

Not until 1990 was Augmented Reality used to describe the live interaction between man and projected graphics. Early uses for VR included US Air Force pilot training programs, and maintenance programs, which helped to track and place items correctly by projecting schematics.

Think back to the last NFL or CFL game you watched? Remember the handy yellow line showing you the First Down Marker? Projected on screen with simple AR technology but now so ubiquitous to the broadcast could you imagine watching a game on TV without it? That piece of technology launched in 1998.

The ARToolkit, an open-source software library, created by Hirozaku Kato in the year 2000, allowed people to combine virtual graphics with real life using video tracking. IN 2009 it was brought to the web. AR has even invaded print when in 2009 readers of Esquire were asked to scan the cover to make Robert Downey Jr leap off the page.

From these humble origins we have made AR such a large part of our lives. From the experimental uses like Google Glasses, to movies and television projecting images in front of us, to apps like PokemonGo showing augmented characters directly on our phone screen.

Where the technology will take us in the future remains to be seen but the future will certainly be exciting.
