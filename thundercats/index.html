Introducing Augmented Reality

Augmented Reality (AR), which is an interactive experience of the real world or a simulated world that bring computer-generated perceptual information to life. AR systems brings to the user a multi-sensory experience which includes visual, audio, haptic, and dimension. The information and its object is overlaid into the real world. AR technologies can be in hardware, display, eyeglasses, HUD, contact lenses, and VRD.

Primarily, AR is grouped into two large groups: mixed reality and computer mediated reality. The purpose of AR is really to enhance the natural environments or situations to offer perceptually enriched experience. Users becomes more interactive and digitally manipulability.

AR is getting popular with iPhone especially iPhone 8 and newer. There are many apps on that App Store that allows users to make AR environments on the fly.

AR is good for gaming, education, and military applications. The benefits of using AR are

  • Cost less
  • Safety
  • Improve real practice experience
  • Less time consuming then setting up a VR world
  • Entertainment and fun

Most Virtual Reality technologies are using AR rendering. They use optical projection systems, monitors, handheld devices, and display systems, like head mounted displays. AR is different than VR. AR provides more freedom for user and more possibilities while VR is more immersive.
