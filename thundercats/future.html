Future of Augmented Reality

The future of AR is currently in toys and games. Why play with an avatar running around on the tv screen shoot aliens when you can be the avatar and the aliens spawn in your living room and you have to turn to shoot them. Which has more WOW, which makes your heart beat a little faster and makes you remember why you loved video games?

Disney, Lego they’re both interested. Layering AR effects on toys, games and action figures is where the world is heading. At this year, 2018, Toy Fair, Hasbro, arguably the world’s largest toy company debuted their first AR toy. It still needs your cellphone to power it but with all the world’s biggest App makers working on AR the possibilities for improvement are amazing.

One of the biggest reason and perhaps why AR will ultimately overtake VR as the “it” technology is cost. Build and app, add to the app store, build a piece of cheap hardware and a user can get an immersive AR experience for under $100 (assuming they already own a current generation smartphone). With ARKit 2 and the new advances where 2 people can share the same Augmented reality on 2 different phones the possibilities for gaming are unlimited. Pong anyone?

Is it all fun and games?

So, it is just entertainment, or can this be a part of an educational purpose. Research is still growing but many say yes. Show kids how to do something in AR reality and see them apply the skills learned in the real world. Sure, some games have little interactivity but the more you stretch them more the app requires of a user the greater the experience.

Excited yet?

So, what’s next? Who knows but gaming, toys, even puzzles may never be the same.
