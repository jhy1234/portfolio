Types of Augmented Reality

There are many different styles and types of Augmented Reality depending on what you’re looking to accomplish with AR. The more popular of the varieties are

  • Marker Based
  • Markerless
  • Projection Based
  • Superimposition Based

Marker Based Augmented Reality

Also known as location recognition, Marker Bases, uses a camera and scans a marker, like a QR Code, to produce a result. QR codes are often used since they can be natively read by many of today’s smartphones and the code is a simple process and needs little processing power to parse into data.

Markerless Augmented Reality

Also known as location based, or GPS based augmented reality, it takes the date from the location of the user. The camera may be used to display information concerning a particular location but may also just project information based on where the user is located. Often used by mapping apps and making use of the native GPS technology of smartphones.

Projection Based Augmented Reality

This works by projecting artificial light onto real world surfaces. The application can sense human interaction with the light, and alter based on that interaction.

Superimposition Based Augmented Reality

This either totally of partially replaces a view of an object. This new augmented projection takes the place on screen of whatever you’re trying to place there. For a practical example, it would be scanning a room and a furniture catalogue and then placing that furniture into the room and seeing how it looks and fits.

Depending on what your needs are there is an Augmented Realty Type to help you out with it.
