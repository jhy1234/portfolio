Tools of AR

Looking to take a chuck of the estimated 83 billion in revue generated from AR? Google, Facebook, Adobe and Apple do, so why not you too.

NThere are many different AR software development kits, depending on what your goal is as a developer we break down the software for you.

First let’s look at the two streams of mobile based AR applications.

Marker based

Maker-based AR is based on image recognition. Scanning a QR code, or pattern and then overlaying digital information on top. The more basic versions need a flat piece of information, like the Esquire cover which had the interactive Robert Downy Jr. While more advanced versions can overly the digital information by scanning a 3D, real life object.

Location-Based

Location-based apps don’t care what the camera is looking at but where the phone/GPS is located. Overlaying a map showing directions to your car. Or the different types of Pokémon that on catches in different locations in PokémonGo.

So which SKD?

Among your first consideration has to be the licence, some cost money, some cost a lot of money and may be out of reach. Apple and Adobe’s ARKit partnership might bring down the cost of some of the paid licences, still too early to tell.

If you’re developing for mobile solely most SDKs support both Apple and Android but if you want to add Windows into the mix you may have to look for a more fully featured SDK. Desktop support is slow, slower then mobile but it’s coming and if you’re looking for universality then that may be another thing to consider.
